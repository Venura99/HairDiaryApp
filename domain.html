Research Project Scope




Literature Survay

Basic mathematics operations and number patterns, which is a vast and intriguing area of mathematics, have a significant psychological impact on developing higher critical thinking, cognitive, and mathematical reasoning skills in primary school children. To improve the mathematics skills of students who are hearing-impaired, interactive sign language learning has been shown to be successful. Use of curricular and assessment modifications, real-world situations, technology, and tailored instruction to meet the unique needs of hearing-impaired students Continuous support, attention, and guidance are essential in the learning process of deaf children. Their individual strengths and weaknesses should be identified, and the learning process should be personalized and updated accordingly. Simultaneously, promoting independence, encouraging them to take on new challenges, and praising and acknowledging their triumphs are important for improving their confidence and self-esteem to establish a quality learning environment



Research Gap

Despite the availability of a variety of desktop applications designed for deaf students in learning sign languages such as American Sign Language (ASL) and British Sign Language (BSL), a major need exists when it comes to developing a dedicated application for teaching mathematics to deaf students who are learning Sinhala Sign Language. An explained lesson plan, multimedia accompaniment, and the ability to assess students' progress through engaging puzzles and quizzes should all be included in such an application. Even though research has been done in Sri Lanka to identify the finger-spelling Sinhala alphabet and several systems have been developed, there is still an evident absence of a mobile-based application designed specifically for the needs of deaf pupils learning sign language. Such an application would enable students to engage in self-study and self-assessment, providing them with valuable insights into their learning progress.



Research Problem

The mathematics poses unique challenges for hearing-impaired students due to its complex mathematics operations, identify Sri Lanka currency, and identify shapes and identify colors on spoken communication. Traditionally, deaf students have relied on sign language to communicate, but this is not a written language and does not provide a direct translation of Sinhala. As a result, many hearing-impaired students struggle to learn the language and fall behind their peers. This can have significant long-term consequences, limiting their ability to pursue further education and employment opportunities. The application developed by researchers is designed to address these challenges by providing hearing-impaired students with a visual aid to learn mathematics. The application uses a combination of images, videos, and interactive activities to help students learn mathematics operations, identify Sri Lanka currency, and identify shapes and identify colors. The application is also designed to be accessible to students with varying levels of hearing loss, using adjustable sound and visual settings to accommodate individual needs.



Research Objectives

This web-based solution has been developed, representing a huge step toward improving educational opportunities for DHH learners in primary schools. The main goal of the project was to develop an exceptional teaching environment that incorporated innovative teaching methods that were highly collaborative. This system's extensive student evaluation system, which supports the effective achievement of learning objectives, is one of its unique features. Additionally, the system keeps track of complete, up-to-date student profiles that offer ongoing insights into each student's academic progress. These carefully crafted profiles empower teachers while also encouraging collaboration between students and parents.



Methodology

The purpose of this research is to develop a tool that can help in the learning of mathematics of deaf children. The Knowledge Inspired Computing (KIC) and Computational Linguistics (CL) foundations of the system. Design an interactive learning environment for teaching numbers, basic mathematical operations, color and shape identification, and basic currency notes, the application should also calculate simple units and measurements and explain numerical patterns.
